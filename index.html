Short Biography

Currently, I am a Ph.D. student in Electronic Science and Technology at Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen.

Before that, in September 2014, I was recommended to pursue a Ph.D. degree at Harbin Institute of Technology without taking the national graduate entrance examination. In the Microelectronics and SoC Research Center, I started my investigation from unmanned aerial vehicles controller design and medical computed tomography analysis, and completed my undergraduate thesis in May 2015 and received the B.S. degree in June 2015. As the last generation of the center, I worked under the supervision of Professor Yan Zhang. Shortly after, my interests had been diverted to the computational nature of vision. After the closure of the center in December 2017, I collaborated with Professor Liyi Xiao from the School of Astronautics, Harbin Institute of Technology and Associate Professor Fei Wang from the Shenzhen Graduate School, Harbin Institute of Technology. After that, I would like to solve some real-world problems, such as object detection and tracking. Now, I am interested in the deep structural models for high-performance visual tracking.

Research Interest

My research interests include: Computer Vision and Computer Architecture.
Especially, I am focusing on:

  • High-performance visual object tracking;
  • CNN model compression and acceleration;
  • FPGA-based acceleration systems for deep learning applications.
